Biography Formula 1

Formula 1, often abbreviated as F1, and also referred to as the "premier class of motor racing" or "the top class of motor racing", is the most popular international motor racing competition. A race is called a Grand Prix and the competition that groups them together is called the "Formula 1 World Championship". Most of the racing circuits where the Grand Prix are held are permanent circuits, although urban circuits are also used. The cars used are single-seaters with the latest technology available; some of these improvements were developed in Formula 1 and ended up being used in commercial cars, such as the disc brake or the turbocharger. The beginning of modern Formula 1 dates back to 1950, in which legendary teams such as Ferrari, Alfa Romeo and Maserati already participated. Some teams have been replaced by new ones that have achieved great successes, such as McLaren, Scuderia Ferrari, Williams or Scuderia AlphaTauri, which have won the world championship several times.
